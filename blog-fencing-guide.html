The Ultimate Fencing Guide

Privacy, security, and style. How to pick the perfect boundary for your property.

Fencing is often an afterthought, but it's one of the most visible parts of your garden. It defines your boundaries, provides security, and sets the backdrop for your planting. With so many options available—from panels to featheredge, wood to concrete—it can be confusing to know what to choose.

Option 1: Featheredge / Closeboard (The Gold Standard)

This is built on-site using vertical boards that overlap (like feathers). It is our most popular choice in Cardiff for rear gardens.

  • Strength: It creates a continuous, solid barrier with no gaps. Extremely strong against high winds.
  • Flexibility: Because it is built from scratch, it can easily follow the slope of uneven ground without "stepping" like panels do.
  • Security: Hard to climb and offers complete privacy.

Option 2: Panel Fencing (Budget & Speed)

Pre-made panels (Lap Panels) slot into posts. This is a cost-effective option and quick to install.

  • Cost: Generally cheaper than featheredge.
  • Maintenance: If a panel breaks in a storm, you can simply lift it out and slide a new one in.
  • Cons: Not as strong as featheredge and can rattle in high winds if not secured properly.

The Big Question: Concrete or Wood Posts?

Concrete Posts

Pros: They will likely outlive you. They don't rot, they don't snap in the wind, and they keep fencing off the damp ground (if using gravel boards).

Cons: More industrial look, heavier to install.

Wooden Posts

Pros: Natural look that blends into the garden. Essential for Featheredge fencing to nail the rails to.

Cons: Even treated timber will eventually rot at the ground level (usually after 10-15 years).

Our Recommendation: For maximum longevity, we recommend concrete posts with Featheredge panels (if using slotted posts) or heavy-duty 4x4 inch treated timber posts properly concreted in with Postcrete.
